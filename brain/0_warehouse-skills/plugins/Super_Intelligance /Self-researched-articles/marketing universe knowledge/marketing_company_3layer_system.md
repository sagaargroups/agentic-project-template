### 📞 DISCOVERY CALL STRUCTURE (Marketing Services)

**Pre-Call Preparation:**
- [ ] Review client's website (built by web agency)
- [ ] Check current Google presence (search their business name)
- [ ] Look at their social media (if any)
- [ ] Research 2-3 competitors
- [ ] Prepare custom insights (1-2 quick wins you noticed)

**Call Agenda (20-30 minutes):**

```
PART 1: UNDERSTANDING BUSINESS (8 minutes)

1. Business Overview
   Q: "Tell me about your business — what do you do and who do you serve?"
   Q: "How long have you been in business?"
   Q: "What makes you different from competitors?"

2. Current Marketing Situation
   Q: "What marketing are you doing right now, if any?"
   Q: "Have you run Google Ads or Facebook Ads before?"
   Q: "Do you have someone managing your social media?"
   Q: "Are you doing any SEO or content marketing?"

3. Goals & Expectations
   Q: "What does success look like for you in the next 6 months?"
   Q: "How many new customers do you want per month?"
   Q: "What's the average value of a customer to your business?"
   Q: "When do you need to see results by?" (Expectation setting!)

PART 2: MARKETING DIAGNOSIS (8 minutes)

4. Current Challenges
   Q: "What's the biggest challenge in getting new customers right now?"
   Q: "Where do most of your customers come from today?"
   Q: "Have you tried marketing before? What worked? What didn't?"

5. Audience & Targeting
   Q: "Who's your ideal customer? (Demographics, location, behavior)"
   Q: "Are you targeting local customers or nationwide?"
   Q: "What problem does your product/service solve for them?"

6. Budget & Timeline
   Q: "What's your budget for marketing per month?"
   Q: "Is this a test budget or committed budget?"
   Q: "When would you like to start?"

PART 3: INSIGHTS & NEXT STEPS (8 minutes)

7. Share Initial Insights
   - "Here's what I noticed about your website..."
   - "Your competitors are doing X, here's an opportunity for you..."
   - "Based on your industry, here's what typically works..."

8. Recommend Services
   - "For your goals, I'd recommend starting with [SERVICE]"
   - "Here's why: [REASON based on their answers]"
   - "Timeline to results: [REALISTIC expectation]"

9. Next Steps
   - "I'll send you a proposal by [DATE]"
   - "It will include: [WHAT'S IN PROPOSAL]"
   - "Any questions before we wrap up?"

PART 4: CLOSE (2 minutes)
   - "Thanks for your time!"
   - "You'll have the proposal within 48 hours"
   - "Let's schedule a follow-up call for [DATE] to review it together"
```

### 📝 MARKETING AUDIT (Optional - For Complex Cases)

**When to do a Marketing Audit:**
- Client has existing marketing (Google Ads, SEO, social media)
- Client wants comprehensive strategy (not just one service)
- Budget > ₹1L/month (worth the extra effort)

**Quick Marketing Audit Checklist (60 minutes):**

```
1. WEBSITE AUDIT (10 min)
   ✓ Page speed (Google PageSpeed Insights)
   ✓ Mobile-friendliness
   ✓ Clear CTAs (call-to-action buttons)
   ✓ Contact forms working
   ✓ SSL certificate active

2. SEO AUDIT (15 min)
   ✓ Google Search Console connected?
   ✓ Google Analytics connected?
   ✓ Title tags optimized?
   ✓ Meta descriptions present?
   ✓ Keyword research done?
   ✓ Backlink profile (Ahrefs free tool)

3. GOOGLE ADS AUDIT (10 min - if running ads)
   ✓ Account structure
   ✓ Quality Score
   ✓ Conversion tracking setup?
   ✓ Wasted spend on irrelevant keywords

4. SOCIAL MEDIA AUDIT (10 min)
   ✓ Profiles complete (bio, contact, link)
   ✓ Posting frequency
   ✓ Engagement rate
   ✓ Follower growth trend

5. COMPETITOR AUDIT (10 min)
   ✓ Top 3 competitors identified
   ✓ Their marketing channels (ads, SEO, social)
   ✓ Their messaging/positioning
   ✓ Opportunities they're missing

6. RECOMMENDATIONS (5 min)
   ✓ Top 3 quick wins
   ✓ Top 3 long-term strategies
   ✓ Budget allocation suggestion
```

**Deliverable:** Simple 2-page PDF with findings + recommendations  
**Tool:** Canva template or Google Docs  
**Time:** 60 minutes  
**Cost to client:** FREE (included in discovery process for high-value prospects)

### 💡 PRICING PRINCIPLES

**1. Setup Fee Logic:**
- Covers: Audit, research, account setup, initial optimization
- One-time only (never charged again unless client leaves and returns)
- Filters out non-serious clients

**2. Monthly Retainer Logic:**
- Recurring revenue = predictable cash flow
- Minimum 3-month commitment (marketing takes time to work)
- Auto-renews unless client cancels (30-day notice)

**3. Performance-Based Pricing (Advanced - Future):**
- Base fee + bonus for hitting targets
- Example: ₹15,000 base + ₹5,000 bonus if we generate 50+ leads
- Only offer this after you've proven you can deliver

**4. Ad Spend Separation:**
- NEVER include ad spend in your fee
- Client pays Google/Meta directly
- You only manage campaigns (charge % of spend OR flat fee)
- Cleaner accounting, no cash flow issues

### 💰 FREELANCER PAYMENT STRUCTURE OPTIONS

**Option 1: Per Task (Recommended for Starting)**
- Pay per deliverable (blog post, ad campaign setup, design)
- Pros: Only pay for completed work, easy to budget
- Cons: Harder to scale, freelancer may prioritize higher-paying clients

**Option 2: Hourly**
- Track time via tools (Toggl, Zoho Projects time tracking)
- Pros: Flexible, pay only for time worked
- Cons: Hard to estimate costs, potential for time padding

**Option 3: Monthly Retainer (Recommended for Scaling)**
- Fixed monthly fee for set number of deliverables
- Example: ₹15,000/month for 4 blog posts + 10 backlinks
- Pros: Predictable costs, freelancer priority access, easier budgeting
- Cons: Risk if freelancer doesn't deliver consistently

**Option 4: Hybrid (Best for Marketing Company)**
- Base retainer + per-project add-ons
- Example: ₹10,000/month base + ₹2,000 per extra blog post
- Pros: Flexibility + predictability
- Cons: Slightly more complex to manage

**Recommended Payment Schedule:**
- Weekly for new freelancers (builds trust)
- Bi-weekly after 1 month of good work
- Monthly after 3 months of consistent delivery
