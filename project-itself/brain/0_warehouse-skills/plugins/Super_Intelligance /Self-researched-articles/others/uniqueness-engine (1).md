# SKILL: Uniqueness Engine

## 🎯 PURPOSE
Ensure every generated prompt produces outputs that are unique, differentiated, and impossible to confuse with generic AI-generated content. This skill is applied to EVERY visual, text, and design prompt to inject specificity and originality.

## 📋 WHEN TO USE
- **ALWAYS** - Apply to every single prompt generated
- Before finalizing any image generation prompt
- Before finalizing any copywriting prompt
- Before finalizing any design/layout prompt
- Before finalizing any video prompt

## 🔧 INPUT REQUIRED
- The draft prompt (before uniqueness applied)
- Business context: type, industry, audience, USP
- Brand feel: tone, style, personality
- What makes THIS business different from competitors

## ⚙️ UNIQUENESS RULES

### **RULE 1: SPECIFICITY OVER GENERICS**

❌ **NEVER USE:**
- "professional looking"
- "modern design"
- "clean and minimal"
- "high quality"
- "engaging content"
- "eye-catching visuals"
- "beautiful website"
- "user-friendly"
- Generic industry terms without context

✅ **ALWAYS USE:**
- Specific visual descriptors (exact colors, compositions, moods)
- Particular emotional responses to trigger
- Concrete scenarios/situations
- Detailed sensory descriptions
- Unexpected combinations

**Example Transformation:**

❌ Generic: "Create a professional hero image for a dental clinic"

✅ Unique: "Create a hero image showing a child's genuine laugh during a dental checkup, sunlight streaming through large windows, focus on trust and comfort rather than clinical perfection. Warm afternoon light, shallow depth of field, capture the moment of connection between dentist and patient. Colors: soft mint green accent walls, natural wood tones, avoid sterile white clinical aesthetic."

---

### **RULE 2: AVOID AI AESTHETIC TROPES**

**Common AI Generated Patterns to AVOID:**

**For Images:**
- Purple/blue gradients on white backgrounds
- Centered subjects with perfect symmetry
- Overly smooth/plastic skin textures
- Generic stock photo poses
- Perfectly arranged objects
- Unrealistic perfection
- Lens flare overuse
- Generic "tech startup" aesthetics

**For Copy:**
- "Welcome to [business name]"
- "We are passionate about..."
- "Your [industry] experts"
- "Solutions for all your needs"
- Exclamation point overuse!!!
- Generic value propositions
- "Empowering" everything
- Corporate buzzword soup

**For Design:**
- Cookie-cutter card layouts
- Standard hamburger menu without personality
- Generic blue CTAs
- Same section spacing everywhere
- Template-feeling navigation
- Predictable footer layouts

**Uniqueness Injection Method:**
1. Identify the generic element
2. Ask: "What would make THIS specific to this business?"
3. Add: Industry detail + Audience emotion + Unexpected element

---

### **RULE 3: INJECT BUSINESS PERSONALITY**

Every prompt must answer: **"Why THIS business and not any other?"**

**Required Personality Injections:**

**A) Industry-Specific Details**
Don't say: "salon services"
Say: "balayage color correction for previously box-dyed hair, specializing in damage repair"

Don't say: "dental care"  
Say: "anxiety-free dentistry for patients with dental phobia, including sedation options"

Don't say: "school education"
Say: "Montessori approach for ages 3-6, emphasis on sensory learning and child-led discovery"

**B) Unique Approach/Philosophy**
Every business has something different. Extract it and weave it into prompts.

Examples:
- Salon: "sustainable, cruelty-free products only, zero-waste color processing"
- Dentist: "same-day crowns, family-friendly environment where kids choose their flavor of fluoride"
- School: "bilingual immersion from age 2, outdoor classroom 3 days per week"
- NGO: "direct aid model, 100% of donations to families, no overhead"

**C) Emotional Signature**
What should someone FEEL encountering this business?

Examples:
- Luxury salon: "pampered, transformed, worth the investment"
- Family dentist: "safe, understood, actually looking forward to visits"
- Progressive school: "excited about learning, confident in their uniqueness"
- Local NGO: "hopeful, connected to real change, part of something bigger"

---

### **RULE 4: UNEXPECTED ELEMENT MANDATE**

Every prompt MUST include at least ONE unexpected element that breaks pattern.

**Methods:**

**A) Contrarian Approach**
What does everyone else in the industry do? Do the opposite.
- Everyone uses blue? Use warm terracotta and sage.
- Everyone shows perfect results? Show the messy, real transformation process.
- Everyone is serious? Inject playful metaphors.

**B) Cross-Industry Inspiration**
Pull visual/conceptual ideas from unrelated fields:
- Dental clinic → hotel hospitality luxury
- School → tech startup innovation
- Salon → art gallery curation
- NGO → documentary filmmaking

**C) Sensory Unexpected**
Add a sensory detail that shouldn't be there but works:
- Visual: "composition that feels like looking through a keyhole"
- Copy: "describe the service using only texture words"
- Layout: "navigation that behaves like flipping through a magazine"

**D) Time/Perspective Shift**
- Show "before the business opened" vs "after"
- Use customer POV instead of business POV
- Capture "the moment right before" the service
- "One year later" perspective

---

### **RULE 5: ANTI-TEMPLATE LANGUAGE**

These phrases indicate generic thinking. Replace them:

| ❌ Template Language | ✅ Unique Replacement |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| "state-of-the-art" | [specific technology/technique name] |
| "best in class" | [concrete measurable distinction] |
| "we offer" | [what client experiences/receives] |
| "our services include" | [transformation they'll undergo] |
| "contact us today" | [specific next step with reason] |
| "modern design" | [exact aesthetic descriptor - brutalist/organic/editorial] |
| "professional team" | [specific expertise + personality] |
| "years of experience" | [what that experience enables them to do] |

---

### **RULE 6: CONTEXT LAYERING**

Add multiple context layers to every prompt:

**Layer 1: Physical Context**
Where does this happen? What's the environment?
"In-clinic experience" → "Small-town dental office, vintage building, original hardwood floors, plants on every windowsill, feels like visiting a friend's well-kept home"

**Layer 2: Temporal Context**  
When is this? What time of day matters?
"Morning appointments" → "Early morning natural light sessions, book the golden hour slot, watch sunrise during your treatment"

**Layer 3: Emotional Context**
What emotional state is the person in?
"New client" → "First-time client nervous about commitment, needs to feel safe making decision, probably comparing options online right now"

**Layer 4: Cultural Context**
What cultural/local elements matter?
"Small town" → "Generational business, everyone knows the owner, reputation matters more than ads"
"Urban millennial" → "Values transparency, researches obsessively, influenced by aesthetic and ethics equally"

---

### **RULE 7: SPECIFICITY CHECKLIST**

Before finalizing ANY prompt, verify:

- [ ] **No generic adjectives:** Replaced "professional" / "modern" / "quality" with specific descriptors
- [ ] **Industry details included:** Used actual terminology from that field
- [ ] **Emotional target clear:** Specified exact feeling to evoke
- [ ] **Visual specificity:** Mentioned actual colors, compositions, lighting, or styles (not just "good looking")
- [ ] **Copy personality:** Voice sounds like a real person, not corporate copy
- [ ] **Unexpected element:** Included at least one pattern-breaking detail
- [ ] **Context-rich:** Layered multiple context dimensions
- [ ] **Anti-template:** Purged all template-sounding phrases

## 📝 TRANSFORMATION EXAMPLES

### **EXAMPLE 1: Image Prompt**

**❌ BEFORE (Generic):**
"Create a professional image of a hair salon interior for the website"

**✅ AFTER (Unique):**
"Create an image of a sustainable hair salon interior during afternoon service. Show one stylist mid-balayage application on a client, visible hand-painted foil work, focus on the concentration and artistry. Space features: reclaimed wood stations, living plant wall behind mirrors, natural light from large industrial windows, visible eco-friendly product line in amber glass bottles. Color palette: warm terracotta, soft sage green, brass fixtures. Composition: slightly off-center, shallow depth of field focusing on the application technique, blurred station mirrors showing multiple stylists in background suggesting busy but not chaotic. Avoid: sterile modern salon aesthetic, posed models, perfect symmetry, stock photo feeling. Capture: working artist atmosphere, sustainability without trying too hard, expertise visible in the technique."

---

### **EXAMPLE 2: Copy Prompt**

**❌ BEFORE (Generic):**
"Write about page copy for a dental clinic"

**✅ AFTER (Unique):**
"Write About page for Riverside Family Dentistry, located in small-town Oregon, established 2015.

Story arc: Dr. Sarah Chen opened this practice because her own childhood dental anxiety wasn't taken seriously. Her philosophy: dentistry should feel more like visiting a wellness spa than a medical office. The entire practice is designed around anxiety reduction.

Unique elements to weave in:
- Weighted blankets available during procedures
- "Flavor bar" where kids choose their fluoride flavor (bubblegum to lavender-mint)
- Noise-canceling headphones with patient's choice of music/podcast
- Treatment rooms have windows overlooking river, not fluorescent overhead lighting
- Same-day crown technology (nobody likes multiple visits)
- Works with patients on payment plans directly, no judgment about insurance gaps

Target audience: Young families (25-40), many first-time parents anxious about their kids' first visits, moderate income, value personal relationships over corporate efficiency.

Emotional tone: Warm, understanding, slightly humorous without being unprofessional. Like talking to a friend who happens to be a dentist. Acknowledge that lots of people hate dentists, position practice as "we get it, we're different."

Length: 350-400 words
Voice: First person plural (we), conversational, specific stories over generic claims
End with: Invitation to visit for a consultation, emphasis on "see if we're a good fit" rather than hard sell
Avoid: Medical jargon, corporate speak, "passion" and "excellence" overuse, anything that sounds like a franchise"

---

### **EXAMPLE 3: Design Prompt**

**❌ BEFORE (Generic):**
"Create a modern, clean website design for a yoga studio"

**✅ AFTER (Unique):**
"Design website for Urban Roots Yoga - yoga studio inside a converted 1920s warehouse, exposed brick, large windows, emphasis on accessibility over instagram-aesthetic.

Theme direction: Anti-minimalist. While most yoga sites go ultra-minimal white/beige, this goes rich and layered. Think: collected over time, personal, not trying to look like every other wellness brand.

Color palette:
- Primary: Deep terracotta (brick building heritage)
- Secondary: Dusty olive green
- Accent: Warm amber
- Background: Cream with slight texture (not pure white)
- Avoid: Millennial pink, sage green (overdone), lavender

Typography:
- Headlines: Editorial serif with personality (not the typical thin sans)
- Body: Comfortable reading font, slightly larger than usual (accessibility focus)
- No all-caps overuse, let words breathe

Layout philosophy: Asymmetric, magazine-style editorial. Break the grid intentionally. Large images that bleed off edges, text overlays that aren't perfectly centered.

Unique elements:
- Homepage: Split hero - left side shows actual diverse students (not models), right side shows warehouse architecture
- Class schedule: Visual calendar not boring list, hover shows instructor photo + what to expect
- Pricing: Transparent, sliding scale shown honestly, community class emphasis
- Interactive element: Virtual tour showing actual studio space (builds trust for first-timers)

What makes this NOT generic:
- Not afraid of color
- Not Instagram-minimal aesthetic
- Shows real people, real space
- Prioritizes accessibility (larger text, clear hierarchy)
- Layout breaks patterns (unexpected scrolling behavior, sections at angles)

Feel: Grounded, inclusive, artistic without being precious, urban without being cold

Absolutely avoid: Stock yoga photos, "namaste" quotes, chakra graphics, spiritual bypassing aesthetics, anything that screams "wellness brand template""

## 💡 USAGE IN WORKFLOW

**When generating prompts in website-lifecycle.md:**

1. Write the basic prompt structure first
2. **Then apply this uniqueness-engine.md** - transform every prompt
3. Run through specificity checklist
4. If it could work for ANY business in that industry → it's not unique enough yet
5. Add one more unexpected element
6. Final check: Would you be excited to use this prompt? Or does it feel like work?

---

## ⚠️ CRITICAL NOTES

- **Uniqueness ≠ Weird:** Unique should feel "exactly right for THIS business" not random or forced
- **Specificity ≠ Limiting:** Detailed prompts actually give AI tools MORE creative direction, not less
- **Context ≠ Fluff:** Every detail should serve the end goal (conversion, trust, differentiation)
- **Breaking patterns ≠ Ignoring best practices:** Be unique within what actually works for user experience

---

## 🎯 GOAL

When this skill is properly applied, the prompt user should think:

*"This prompt is SO specific to this business that if I use it, there's no way the output will look generic or like it came from a template."*

The end outputs (images, copy, designs) should be **impossible to confuse** with generic AI-generated content or template websites.
