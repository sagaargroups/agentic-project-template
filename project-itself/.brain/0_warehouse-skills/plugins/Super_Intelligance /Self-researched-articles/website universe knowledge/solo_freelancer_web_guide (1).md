## Your Situation
- **Solo operator** (no team)
- **Beginner-level** (understand code structure, libraries, basic logic)

## Part 2: YOUR TECH STACK (What You Actually Need)

### Core Tools (Keep It Simple!)

**Website Builder/CMS:**
1. **WordPress** + Elementor (easiest for beginners)
   - Pre-built templates available
   - Drag-and-drop editing
   - Client can update content themselves
   - Cost: $0 (WordPress) + $59/year (Elementor Pro)

2. **Webflow** (if you want to learn modern tool)
   - Beautiful templates
   - No code needed for most things
   - Cost: $14-29/month per site

3. **Framer** (trending in 2026, great for portfolios)
   - AI-assisted design
   - Fast export to production
   - Cost: $5-15/month per site

**🎯 RECOMMENDATION FOR YOU: Start with WordPress + Elementor**
- Lowest learning curve
- Most local clients know WordPress
- Huge community for help
- Thousands of free templates

**Day 8-14: Local Outreach (Do ALL of These!)**

**A. In-Person Cold Calling** (Most Effective!)
- Print 50 business cards ($20 at Vistaprint)
- Visit 5-10 businesses per day in your city
- Target: Restaurants, salons, gyms, retail stores
- Script: "Hi, I'm [Name], I build websites for local businesses. I noticed [Business] isn't showing up well on Google. Can I leave my card with the owner?"
- **Success Rate: 1-3% = 1-3 leads from 100 visits**

**B. Facebook/Instagram DMs** (Second Most Effective)
- Find local businesses with bad/no websites
- Send friendly DM: "Hey! Love your [product/service]. I build websites for local businesses. Would you be interested in a free website audit?"
- **Success Rate: 0.5-1% = 5-10 leads from 1000 DMs**

**C. Local Facebook Groups**
- Join 10-20 local business groups
- Post weekly: "Web designer offering discounted rates to [city] businesses this month. DM me if interested!"
- **Success Rate: 1-2 leads per week**

**D. Google My Business Scraping**
- Search "restaurants near me", "salons near me"
- Check which ones have bad/no websites
- Call or email them directly
- **Success Rate: 2-5% response rate**

**E. Friends & Family**
- Tell everyone you know
- Offer first 3 clients 50% off
- **Success Rate: Usually get 1-2 projects**

### PHASE 2: DISCOVERY CALL (15-30 minutes)

**Your Goal:** Qualify client + understand needs + close the deal

**Questions to Ask:**
1. "What does your business do?" (understand their service)
2. "Do you currently have a website?" (check if redesign or new)
3. "What's your main goal?" (leads, sales, credibility, information)
4. "What's your budget?" (qualify if they can afford you)
5. "When do you need this done?" (urgency = higher price)
6. "Who are your competitors?" (check their websites for ideas)

**Closing Script:**
"Based on what you've told me, I'd recommend [Package Name] for $[Price]. This includes [list features]. I can have this ready in [X] days. Does that work for you?"

**If They Say "Too Expensive":**
- Offer payment plan: "I can do 50% upfront, 50% on delivery"
- Reduce scope: "We can start with 5 pages, add more later"
- Emphasize ROI: "If this brings you just 2-3 new customers, it pays for itself"

**Get These Before Call Ends:**
1. ✅ Verbal "Yes" to move forward
2. ✅ Collect deposit (50% upfront via PayPal/Stripe)
3. ✅ Schedule content collection meeting
4. ✅ Send contract/proposal email immediately after

### PHASE 4: PRODUCTION (Day 2-7)

#### DAY 2: SETUP & STRUCTURE (2-3 hours)

**Step 1: Domain & Hosting**
- Buy domain at Namecheap ($8-12/year) - charge client $50
- Get hosting: Hostinger ($2-3/month) or SiteGround ($3-6/month) - charge client $150-300/year
- Install WordPress + SSL certificate

**Step 2: Choose Template**
- Go to Envato Elements ($16/month for unlimited templates)
- Search for [client's industry] + "WordPress theme"
- Download and install theme
- **Time: 30 minutes**

**Step 3: Install Required Plugins**
```
Essential Plugins (free):
- Elementor (page builder)
- Yoast SEO (SEO optimization)
- WPForms (contact forms)
- Really Simple SSL (security)
- UpdraftPlus (backups)
- WP Super Cache (speed)
```

**Step 4: Create Site Structure**
- Create all pages (blank for now):
  - Homepage
  - About Us
  - Services (or Products)
  - Gallery (if needed)
  - Contact
  - Privacy Policy (use generator: termsfeed.com)

#### DAY 5-6: DESIGN & BUILD (6-8 hours)

**Using Elementor (Drag-and-Drop):**

**Hour 1-2: Homepage**
- Add hero section with AI-generated image
- Headline + subheadline from AI-generated copy
- CTA button ("Get Quote", "Call Now", "Learn More")
- Services overview section (3-4 boxes with icons)
- About preview section
- Testimonials section (make 2-3 fake ones initially)
- Contact section with form

**Hour 3-4: Inner Pages**
- About page: Add AI content, team photos
- Services pages: Copy AI text, add relevant images
- Gallery: Add AI-generated or client photos
- Contact page: WPForms contact form + Google Maps embed

**Hour 5-6: Styling & Polish**
- Apply brand colors (or choose nice palette: coolors.co)
- Ensure mobile responsive (check on phone simulator)
- Add transitions/animations (subtle)
- Test all links

**Hour 7-8: SEO Basics**
- Yoast SEO setup:
  - Page titles (include city + service keywords)
  - Meta descriptions (use AI to generate)
  - Alt text for all images
  - Submit sitemap to Google Search Console

**Checklist:**
- [ ] All links work
- [ ] Contact form sends emails (test it!)
- [ ] Mobile responsive on phone/tablet

## Part 11: RED FLAGS TO AVOID (Save Yourself Headaches!)

### BAD CLIENT WARNING SIGNS:

❌ **"Can you do it for free? I'll pay you when I make money"**
→ RUN AWAY. They won't pay.

❌ **"I have a huge project, but start with this small thing first"**
→ The "huge project" never comes. They're testing if you work for free.

❌ **"My budget is $200 for a 20-page website"**
→ They don't value your work. Politely decline.

❌ **Asks for unlimited revisions**
→ Set boundary: "Package includes 2 rounds of revisions. Additional rounds are $100 each"

❌ **Won't pay deposit**
→ "No deposit, no work" - this is standard practice

❌ **Constantly changes mind / adds features**
→ Scope creep! Say: "That's outside our agreement. I can quote that separately"

❌ **Rude or disrespectful**
→ Fire them. No amount of money is worth the stress

---

### GOOD CLIENT GREEN FLAGS:

✅ Responds to emails quickly
✅ Has content/photos ready
✅ Pays deposit immediately
✅ Refers to you as "expert" / trusts your judgment
✅ Realistic timeline expectations
✅ Open to ongoing maintenance relationship
